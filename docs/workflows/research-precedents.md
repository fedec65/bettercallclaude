## Best Practices

### ‚úÖ Do:

1. **Start Broad, Then Narrow**: Begin with general topic search, refine based on results
2. **Verify All Citations**: Always verify BGE citations against bundesgericht.ch
3. **Note Precedent Hierarchy**: BGE > Cantonal courts > Administrative authorities
4. **Track Recent Developments**: Focus on recent precedents (last 5-10 years)
5. **Document Your Research**: Save queries and responses for future reference
6. **Cross-Reference Languages**: Use multi-lingual search to find additional precedents

### ‚ùå Don't:

1. **Over-Rely on Summaries**: Read full BGE decisions for critical cases
2. **Ignore Cantonal Law**: Federal law doesn't cover everything
3. **Skip Citation Verification**: Unverified citations can undermine legal arguments
4. **Neglect Doctrinal Commentary**: BGE precedents should be read with scholarly analysis
5. **Forget Update Checks**: Law evolves - verify precedents are still good law

## Tips for Efficient Research

### Speed Optimization

1. **Use Abbreviations**: "BGE on Art. 97 OR" instead of full sentences
2. **Parallel Research**: Open multiple queries simultaneously
3. **Template Queries**: Save common query patterns
4. **Citation Shortcuts**: "BGE 144-III-93" works as well as full format

### Quality Enhancement

1. **Read Full Decisions**: For critical cases, review full BGE text
2. **Check Dissents**: Note dissenting opinions for nuanced understanding
3. **Follow Citations**: Review cases cited within BGE decisions
4. **Cross-Verify**: Use multiple sources for important holdings

### Organization

1. **Create Research Folders**: Organize by topic/client/matter
2. **Save Query History**: Document your research path
3. **Build Citation Library**: Maintain frequently-used precedents
4. **Track Updates**: Set alerts for new BGE decisions in your practice areas
