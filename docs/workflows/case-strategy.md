## Strategic Decision Framework

### When to Litigate

âœ… **Proceed with Litigation** if:
- Probability of success >70% on liability
- Expected value >2x litigation costs
- Non-economic factors support litigation
- Settlement negotiations failed at reasonable value
- Precedent-setting value for client's business

### When to Settle

âœ… **Pursue Settlement** if:
- Foreseeability or other significant legal risks
- Litigation costs approach expected recovery
- Business relationship worth preserving
- Defendant has credible defenses
- Time to judgment problematic for client

### When to Walk Away

âœ… **Abandon Claim** if:
- Probability of success <30%
- Expected value negative
- Defendant judgment-proof
- Costs exceed maximum possible recovery
- Reputational risks outweigh benefits

## Best Practices

### âœ… Do:

1. **Quantify Everything**: Convert qualitative assessments to probabilities
2. **Update Continuously**: Reassess strategy as new evidence emerges
3. **Consider Costs**: Factor in all litigation expenses
4. **Think Long-Term**: Evaluate precedent and relationship impacts
5. **Document Decisions**: Record strategic choices for client communication

### âŒ Don't:

1. **Over-Optimize**: Don't chase marginal probability improvements
2. **Ignore Emotions**: Client psychology matters in settlement
3. **Neglect Alternatives**: Always consider non-litigation options
4. **Underestimate Costs**: Litigation always costs more than estimated
5. **Forget Business Context**: Legal wins may be business losses

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Strong Case, Weak Defendant

**Situation**: High probability of success but defendant financially unstable

**Strategy**:
- Pursue preliminary asset seizure (Art. 271 ZPO)
- Accelerate proceedings
- Prioritize early settlement before insolvency
- Consider partial settlement for immediate payment

### Scenario 2: Weak Case, Sympathetic Facts

**Situation**: Legal case marginal but facts favor client

**Strategy**:
- Emphasize factual narrative in pleadings
- Request lay judge participation if available
- Prepare strong opening statement for oral hearing
- Highlight equitable considerations (Art. 4 ZGB)

### Scenario 3: Complex Case, High Stakes

**Situation**: Novel legal issues, significant amount in controversy

**Strategy**:
- Invest in comprehensive legal research
- Consider expert witnesses for complex issues
- Build detailed evidentiary record
- Prepare for potential appeals (BGE precedent-setting)
